Howson was CEO from 2012 until July 2017 and is the only Carillion director confirmed disqualified on the register at time of search. He agreed a disqualification undertaking in October 2023 — over five years after the collapse — which is the standard trajectory for Insolvency Service investigations of this scale.

## 5. Summary Risk Assessment

| Category | Finding | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Company status | In compulsory liquidation since Jan 2018 | **Critical** |
| Outstanding charges | Yes | **High** |
| Filing compliance | Accounts and confirmation statement both overdue | **High** |
| Gazette notices | 50+ liquidator appointments across the group | **Critical** |
| Disqualified directors | CEO Richard Howson — 8-year ban (2023–2031) | **High** |
| Other executives | Chairman, interim CEO, CFO — no current disqualifications recorded | Noted |

**Bottom line:** Carillion PLC is one of the largest corporate insolvencies in UK history. The entire group is in liquidation, all filings are overdue, and the former CEO carries a formal disqualification order. Any counterparty or supply chain exposure to this group should be treated as unrecoverable. The Insolvency Service investigation remains active — further disqualifications of other executives remain possible.
